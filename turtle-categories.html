Turtle categories in Sri Lanka

"Slow and Steady, Turtle Categories Lead the Way in Sri Lanka!"

Leatherback Turtles

Physical Description

Leatherback turtles, scientifically known as Dermochelys coriacea, are a remarkable species of sea turtles that possess several unique characteristics. They are renowned for their enormous size, with individuals reaching lengths of up to 6 to 7 feet and weighing between 550 and 2,000 pounds. What sets them apart from other turtles is their distinctive shell, which is not composed of hard, bony scutes like their counterparts but rather consists of a layer of leathery skin with longitudinal ridges.

Olive Ridley Turtles

Physical Description

The Olive Ridley turtle, scientifically known as Lepidochelys olivacea, Olive Ridley turtles are small to medium-sized compared to other sea turtle species. They typically measure around 2 to 2.5 feet (60 to 75 centimeters) in shell length and weigh between 75 to 100 pounds (34 to 45 kilograms). Their shells, or carapaces, are characterized by a heart-shaped appearance and olive-green coloration, which gives them their name.

Hawksbill Turtles

Physical Description

The Hawksbill turtle, scientifically known as Eretmochelys imbricata, Hawksbill turtles are small to medium-sized sea turtles, with adults typically measuring between 2.5 to 3 feet (75 to 90 centimeters) in shell length and weighing around 100 to 150 pounds (45 to 68 kilograms). They have a distinctive tapered beak-like snout, which resembles a hawk's beak, hence their name. Their shells, or carapaces, are known for their intricate patterns and are composed of overlapping scutes.

Green Turtles

Physical Description

Green turtles, scientifically known as Chelonia mydas, Green turtles are one of the largest species of sea turtles, with adults measuring around 3 to 4 feet (1 to 1.2 meters) in carapace length and weighing between 200 to 500 pounds (90 to 230 kilograms) on average. They have a streamlined body and a unique teardrop-shaped carapace, which is typically shades of brown, olive, or black. Despite their name, their skin and shell are not necessarily green.

Loggerhead Turtles

Physical Description

Loggerhead turtles, scientifically known as Caretta caretta, Loggerhead turtles are the largest hard-shelled turtles and can grow to an average length of around 3 to 4 feet (0.9 to 1.2 meters) and weigh between 200 to 350 pounds (90 to 159 kilograms). They have a reddish-brown or yellow-brown carapace (shell) with a heart-shaped appearance and powerful jaws.
